Success is fragile in many ways; perfection is an illusion. We must therefore act ahead of certainty, remembering *...faith without works is dead (James 2:17)*[^2]. 

At inflection points, faith becomes vigilance made visible—relentless work carried out in strategic dissonance: *“I believe; help my unbelief” (Mark 9:24)*[^3]. Kierkegaard described this as the courage to act without guarantees,[^4] while Nietzsche warned that survival belongs to those disciplined enough to endure tension.[^5] 

That tension is not merely a catalyst for growth—it is the condition for survival. Only the paranoid survive.
